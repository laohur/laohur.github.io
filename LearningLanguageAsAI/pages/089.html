Hammer

A hammer is a tool, most often a hand tool, consisting of a weighted "head" fixed to a long handle that is swung to deliver an impact to a small area of an object. ã€€This can be, for example, to drive nails into wood, to shape metal (as with a forge), or to crush rock. ã€€Hammers are used for a wide range of driving, shaping, breaking and non-destructive striking applications. ã€€Traditional disciplines include carpentry, blacksmithing, warfare, and percussive musicianship (as with a gong). ã€€Hammering is use of a hammer in its strike capacity, as opposed to prying with a secondary claw or grappling with a secondary hook. ã€€Carpentry and blacksmithing hammers are generally wielded from a stationary stance against a stationary target as gripped and propelled with one arm, in a lengthy downward planar arcâ€”downward to add kinetic energy to the impactâ€”pivoting mainly around the shoulder and elbow, with a small but brisk wrist rotation shortly before impact;
